WHAT IS A HEART ATTACK?

  • Heart attack occurs as a result of fat accumulation within the arteries which supply the heart muscle.
  • These fat accumulation are called as plaque. If these plaque break open, clot develops at the site causing complete closure of the artery resulting in a heart attack.
  • Presence of Diabetes, hypertension, obesity, smoking, sedentary life, unhealthy dietetic practices, Post menopausal state, genetic factors are some of the factors which increases the risk of an individual to heart attack.

CAN WOMEN GET HEART ATTACK?


  • Yes!!! Women can also develop heart attack especially in post- menopausal age group.
Can it occur in earlier age group?
  • Yes!! If they have added risk factors and especially if they are smokers.
CAN YOUNG INDIVIDUALS GET HEART ATTACK?
  • Yes!!! Especially individuals with genetic risk factors and in our country - in smokers.

WHAT ARE THE WARNING SIGNS OF A HEART ATTACK?

  • Uncomfortable pressure, squeezing, fullness or pain in the center of your chest. It lasts more than a few minutes, or goes away and comes back.
  • Pain or discomfort in one or both arms, your back, neck, jaw or stomach.
  • Shortness of breath with or without chest discomfort.
  • Other signs such as breaking out in a cold sweat, nausea or lightheadedness.

WHAT SHOULD I DO IF I AM HAVING WARNING SIGNS OF A HEART ATTACK?


  • It always helps to have the nearest hospital s ambulance number.
  • A basic ECG is a must to rule out a heart attack in most cases. If the first ECG is normal and still symptoms are persisting after taking antacids it is important to repeat an ECG after an hour or two.
  • Blood tests to rule out heart attack- CKMB , Trop I/ Trop T can be done in hospitals with available facilities.
  • Keep your medical records in accessible place. Please carry them even at emergencies. It will help in faster diagnosis.
  • Most people these days have android or iOS phones. It would be advisable to have a file of images of health records stored in the phone.
  • Always carry any insurance card and Aadhar card to the hospital for ease of admission and treatment.

HOW IS A HEART ATTACK TREATED IN HOSPITAL?

  • The presence of heart attack can be confirmed by one and or all of the three- ECG, Cardiac enzymes- a blood test, ECHO.
  • Patient is triaged whether they require immediate intervention or medical line of treatment.
  • Medical line of treatment involves administering blood thinners- which are potent medicines which dissolve the clot which cause obstruction in the coronaries?
  • In patients needing operative procedure, an immediate coronary angiogram is done and subsequently based on the angiographic technical details the line of therapy is chosen-Medical treatment versus stenting/ angioplasty versus CABG. The treating doctor discusses the best option based on the age, clinical condition, associated co-morbidities and angiographic results.

Do all patients after a heart attack require an angiogram?

  • An angiogram is a basic interventional test which helps to triage the patient into different treatment plan.It may be deferred in select cases.

WHAT IS THE PROGNOSIS AFTER A HEART ATTACK?

Heart attack/ Acute myocardial infarction (MI) is associated with a 30% mortality rate ( death rate); about 50% of the deaths occur prior to arrival at the hospital.


What is the mortality rate in hospital for a patient who is admitted with heart attack?
  • Despite all the right treatment, there is 5-10% rate of in hospital death in a patient with heart attack which is largely dependent on the age, gender, extent of heart attack, pumping function of the heart and whether patient received revascularisation, whether patient had additional complications related to heart attack.

HOW CAN I REDUCE MY RISK FOR A HEART ATTACK?


  • Don’t smoke, and avoid second-hand smoke.
  • Treat high blood pressure, if you have it.
  • Eat a heart-healthy diet that’s low in saturated and trans fats, sodium (salt) and added sugars.
  • Get at least 150 minutes of moderate- intensity physical activity a week.
  • Reach and maintain a healthy weight.
  • Control your blood sugar if you have diabetes.
  • See your doctor for regular check-ups.
  • Take your medicines exactly as prescribed.

UNCERTAINTIES AFTER A HEART ATTACK- PSYCHOLOGICAL ASPECTS

Most patients say they have unpleasant feelings after a heart attack. These are normal and easy to understand. It’s a good idea to talk to someone about your feelings — don’t keep them inside. In time, these feelings should go away.

Fear: of dying, of chest pains, that you can’t have sex ,that you can’t work

Anger: that it happened to you, at family and friends

Depression : such as thinking... .“Life is over.” You might not be the same again.Others might think you are weak.


How will my family feel?

People who are close to you will also “feel” your heart attack. Instead of keeping bad feelings in, you should all talk about them.


Family members may feel...

  • Frightened to see you in the hospital.
  • Angry that the heart attack came at a bad time.
  • Guilty because they think they “caused” it, even if they know it’s not possible.

What changes should I make?

  • Get help to quit if you smoke.
  • Manage high blood pressure.
  • Eat healthy meals low in saturated fat, trans fat, sodium (salt) and added sugars.Get involved in regular physical activities.
  • Reach and maintain a healthy weight.
  • Take your medicine exactly as prescribed.

What about sex?

  • Check with your doctor first, but you should be able to have sex the way you did before. You should be ready when you’re able to walk around easily.
  • If you have chest pain during sex, have lost interest, or are worried about having sex, talk with your doctor.

When can I go back to work?

  • Most people go back to work in two weeks to three months.
  • Your doctor may have you take tests to find out if you can do the kind of work you did before.
  • Some people may have to change jobs to make it easier on their heart.
