WHEN TO START EXERCISES AFTER A HEART ATTACK?

Usually 10- 14 days after a heart attack, walking on level ground may be started and can be built up gradually.

Simple stretching and home exercises are a good idea until your cardiac rehabilitation comes in to play 4 - 6 weeks after your heart attack.

Use how you feel to gauge how well you are coping and move things on when you can manage your set tasks easily.

  • Avoid activity within 2 hours of a bath or a heavy meal.
  • Don't do physical activity if you are tired at the end of the day. Wait until the morning or choose the time of day you feel at your most refreshed and relaxed.
  • Take your time.

Keep a daily diary
It can be very helpful to keep a daily diary of your home exercises and activities. This will allow you and your doctor / nurse to see your progress.

  • Join an exercise group if you lack motivation to do it alone.
  • If you miss a day, plan to make up for it the next day.
  • Don’t overdo; with moderate exercises you should be able to talk comfortably during exercise.
  • Don’t compare your fitness routine to another’s.
  • Warm up, cool down and stretch. Your walk should be slower at the start and as you finish. Once you've completed the workout, be sure to stretch.
  • Avoid too cold and too hot weather. For the sake of your heart, exercise in mild weather or indoors.

HOW TO START PHYSICAL ACTIVITY?


  • Start slowly and build up gradually.
  • Aim for 90 -150 minutes of moderate intensity exercise per week. Try to do strengthening of muscles two days a week.
  • Wear comfortable clothes and shoes during exercise.
  • Drink water before, during and after exercise unless you have been specifically asked by your doctor to restrict water intake.
  • Alternate the exercises- walking/ jogging/ cycling/ yoga.
  • Try to stick to a schedule.

WHAT NOT TO DO?


Do not exercise in the presence of chest pain

You must stop any exercise if you feel chest pain or become too breathless to talk. Rest for a few minutes and then start again if you feel better but go more slowly.

A few particular actions, which are still risky for you to try, because they put a sudden and unusual strain on your heart.

  • Lifting or pushing heavy weights.
  • Straining with all your strength as in pushing a car .
  • Exercising until you are too breathless to talk.
  • Making short, heavy, sharp efforts like digging.
