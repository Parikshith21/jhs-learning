WHAT IS A CORONARY ANGIOGRAM?

  • Coronaries are names of arteries which supply the heart muscle.
  • There are conventionally three major coronaries.
  • These blood vessels can get blocked with clot formation or build up of fat, which can result in heart attack.
  • Coronary angiogram is a specialised x ray test done as in-patient procedure by trained specialists i.e. cardiologists.

WHAT ARE ANTIPLATELETS? WHY IS IT NECESSARY IN A PATIENT WITH CORONARY ARTERY DISEASE?


  • Antiplatelets ( aspirin/ clopidogrel/ ticagrelor/ prasugrel. are medicines prescribed by your doctor to prevent clot formation inside the blood vessels which can cause heart attack or stroke.
Which of these should be avoided if a patient has had a prior stroke?
  • Prasugrel. It is important to inform your health care provider.
How long should I take the medication?
  • Treatment will be tailored to the needs and indication in a given patient.
  • Patient with coronary artery disease will be on one or the other antiplatelet for life.

WHAT IS A STENT?

Stents help keep coronary arteries open and reduce the chance of a heart attack.


When a stent is used, it’s collapsed and put over the balloon catheter. It’s then moved into the area of the blockage. When the balloon is inflated, the stent expands, locks in place and forms a scaffold.


This holds the artery open. The stent stays in the artery permanently and holds it open. This improves blood flow to the heart muscle and relieves symptoms (usually chest pain).

WHAT IS CORONARY ANGIOPLASTY?


Angioplasty opens blocked arteries and restores normal blood flow to your heart muscle. It is not major surgery. It is done by threading a catheter (thin tube) through a small puncture in a leg or arm artery to the heart. The blocked artery is opened by inflating a tiny balloon in it.


A metallic wire mesh tube called stent is placed.


There are two types of stent. Medicated and non- medicated. Medicated stents are mostly used.

The stent remains within the patient s blood vessel and normal lining of blood vessel grows over it.


When will I be discharged from the hospital after an angioplasty?
  • Usually 2-3 days later if everything is uneventful; longer stay may be needed in sick patients and unstable patients.

Do I have to take medications after an angioplasty?
  • Antiplatelets are a group of medications which must be taken after an angioplasty procedure to prevent the stent from getting blocked; the choice of anti platelet and duration of therapy shall be decided on case to case basis depending on several technical, anatomical, medical and social factors.

Once stenting is done, will I never develop a block later?
  • The stenting procedure that is done is to tackle the existing block. It does not entail that future blocks may not occur. Lifestyle modification, medication compliance, regular follow up s, healthy diet and exercise helps in preventing further blocks.

WHEN SHOULD YOU IMMEDIATELY SEEK TREATMENT WITHOUT DELAY AFTER AN ANGIOPLASTY?

When there are warning signs such as
  • Worsening chest discomfort,
  • Worsening shortness of breath,
  • Loss of consciousness
  • Bleeding at the site of procedure
  • Gross swelling at the site of procedure
  • Spontaneous skin bleeds which can appear as dark purple- blue rash.
  • Frank bleeding in the stools or urine; black tarry stools.

WHEN SHOULD YOU FOLLOW UP AFTER AN ANGIOPLASTY

Post angioplasty most patients will be asked to come after 15 days for a follow up. You may be asked to come earlier if the doctor feels it is pertinent.

What are the things that will be done at a follow up visit?
  • Clinical assessment shall be done. Dose of medications especially antiplatelets, antidiabetics, antihypertensives and diuretics may be adjusted.
  • Activity plan to get back to normal working pattern shall be suggested. Usually most patients can resume their normal working routine at the end of four weeks.

WHAT SHOULD I COME PREPARED FOR DURING FOLLOW UPS




  • Specific investigations suggested at previous visit or discharge has to be done at follow ups. It will be of considerable ease for treatment adjustment if patients come for follow ups on pre- specified days of the unit under which they were previously admitted.
  • In emergencies, patients can come at any time and hour of the day to the emergency room.
  • Please carry the discharge summary and the current treatment details.

WHAT IS ECHOCARDIOGRAPHY?

Echo cardiography is heart ultrasound, a simple and painless procedure that takes “moving pictures” of the heart with sound waves.

  • Echo tests are done by trained sonographers.
  • No X-rays will be used.
  • It’s painless and has no side effects.
  • It can be done even in pregnancy.

WHAT IS TMT( TREADMILL TEST) OR STRESS TEST?


Why do people need stress tests?

Doctors use exercise stress tests to find out:

  • If your symptoms (such as chest pain or difficulty breathing) are related to your heart.
  • How hard you should exercise when you are joining a cardiac rehabilitation program or starting an exercise program.
  • If treatments you have received for heart disease are working.
  • If you need other tests (such as a coronary angiogram) to detect narrowed arteries.
  • If you have an irregular heartbeat.

What is monitored during the test?

  • Your heart rate
  • Your breathing
  • Your blood pressure
  • Your electrocardiogram (ECG or EK)
  • How tired you feel
